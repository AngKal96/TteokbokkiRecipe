Welcome back to G's Kitchen! Today we will be making a very simple and spicy Korean street food: Tteokbokki! Tteokbokki, or simmered rice cake, is a popular Korean food made from small-sized garae-tteok (long, white, cylinder-shaped rice cakes) called tteokmyeon. Eomuk (fish cakes), boiled eggs, and scallions are some common ingredients paired with tteokbokki in dishes. It can be seasoned with either spicy gochujang (chili paste) or non-spicy ganjang (soy sauce)-based sauce. Tteokbokki is commonly purchased and eaten at bunsikjip (snack bars) as well as pojangmacha (street stalls).
For our home-cooked meal today, this recipe I have modified it to fit my tastes and can be made in under 45 minutes. Based on your preferences, you can add more than what is mentioned in the recipe. I sometimes like to add pork belly in mine, though I have also added in vegetables like sliced carrots and cabbage from time to time. Give this recipe a try, you will not regret it.
